## Three findings worth keeping

1. **The watch-list threat downgrades MEDIUM → LOW.** These aren't Archero-adjacent at all; they're Epic Seven / AFK Arena competitors. Report §4's "watch for weapon/draft features" stays as cheap insurance, but only **Terbis** is worth a glance (Webzen-funded + it's the one with a roguelite touch — see below). OZ Re:write is off the list (dead).

2. **OZ Re:write shut down after 8 months.** A concrete churn data point: the crowded anime-gacha-JRPG pool is **brutal** — even a HYBE-backed, polished Blue-Archive-style title died inside a year. This is the pool your *skin* could get you mis-bucketed into (Report §3). Reinforces: stay out of it by positioning, not just by design.

3. **Terbis's "Dream Labyrinth" is the only roguelite touchpoint in the entire cluster — and it's a bolt-on side-mode.** It offers a 3-relic pick after victories. That is the single closest any of these games gets to WeaponCraft's between-wave draft. The tell: even when an anime-collector studio dabbles in roguelite-draft, they ship it as **optional secondary content**, never the core loop. That *validates* WeaponCraft's bet — **a roguelite-arena-as-the-whole-game in the anime space is still uncontested.**

So that specific footage may actually be Terbis or an unidentified title. **It does not change the verdict** — both candidates are unrelated hero-collectors — but treat the per-game *frame* mapping as approximate; the *design* findings come from authoritative web sources, not the reel.
