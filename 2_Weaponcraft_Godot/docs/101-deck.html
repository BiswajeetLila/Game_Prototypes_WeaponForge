§3 · Core thesis

The idea, in player voice

"I command three heroes who hold the line in an arena while monsters pour in — they auto-fight with whatever weapons I've equipped, and I tap each hero to fire their ultimate. The twist is what I collect: I don't pull heroes, I pull weapons. My seven heroes are locked, each with their own story and personality, but the blades, staves and runes they wield come from a slot-machine forge. Between waves I draft skill cards that visibly transform my abilities — split a storm cyclone into three, chain my hellfire to the next enemy. Five-minute runs. The heroes are mine; the weapons are the world's."

§4 · Gameplay journey · D1 first 60s

The opening minute

  • 0–5s. Studio logo → 5s cinematic: Bran the warrior raises his anime-styled sword in a dim chamber. "Hold the line." Tap to start.
  • 5–15s. Tutorial wave 1, no menus. Bran in the center-left of a side-view arena. Slimes waddle in from the right. Heroes auto-attack. Damage numbers pop; slimes burst into XP gems. Wave clears in ~8s.
  • 15–25s. Forge Draft modal slides up. Three cards: +20% atk speed, +200% HP, Storm Cyclone splits into 3. Tooltip highlights the third. Player taps.
  • 25–40s. Wave 2 — Bran's next swing conjures three cyclones instead of one. Visible cause-and-effect. Elara the mage drops into the back slot.
  • 40–60s. Wave 3 — Vex the rogue joins. By the wave-5 Slime King boss, the squad fires ultimates and wins at half HP. Reward: "Forge Wheel pull available!" — the "I want to forge my own weapon" moment.

§5 · Hypothesis

Why this would work

Audience pain

Genre's two unmet wants

Wittle / Archero 2 mid-core grads have a loop that works ($21M/mo Wittle), but: (1) heroes are interchangeable art with no story; (2) the collection thrill is hero-shaped, forcing endless gacha sprawl + predatory whale tail.

The bet

Invert the gacha axis

Equipment-is-gacha + heroes-not-pulled is precedented — Archero shipped it to $263M lifetime. What's unprecedented in our 50-game scan: hard-lock the roster to deterministic story progression, give each of 7 heroes Honkai-grade personality + mission chain + portrait evolution.

Moat

The combination

Habby can bolt a weapon banner onto Wittle in a season patch. They cannot story-lock heroes without forking their hero-banner LTV model. The combination is the moat; the validation is Wittle's slot-level retention loop.

§6 · Risks

The single fragile assumption

Wittle's mid-core mobile audience will accept weapons-as-gacha replacing heroes-as-gacha — because the combination of equipment-gacha (precedented) + story-locked roster (unprecedented) preserves Wittle's slot-level retention loop while adding hero-narrative depth competitors don't have.

If it breaks: players feel their locked heroes are "not premium" OR weapon pulls don't carry the dopamine of a hero pull → both anchors dilute → game falls back to "Wittle with worse art."

Stage-1 dual-anchor SSR probe: "Pulling weapons feels as exciting as pulling heroes, AND I feel attached to my heroes despite not pulling them" — must score ≥6/10 on BOTH axes simultaneously. Else pivot to a hybrid (some heroes pullable).

Re-tests: Stage-4 gameplay video re-tests the forge-pull splash. Phase-1 50-player playtest measures D1 parity with Wittle.

§8 · Progression

The D1 → D14 climb

Kept vague on purpose — Stage 2 SSR will pressure-test the cadence.

D1

3 heroes, ~3 weapons, first Forge Draft transform seen, 4 shadow roster slots visible.

"What else can I forge?" + the visibly empty roster grid.

D3

4–5 heroes (Hot Paladin joined), daily Boss Rush unlocked, first hero personal-mission quest.

Element-weakness boss-of-the-day → smart squad/weapon swaps.

D7

5 heroes, first Catalyst compound (Firestorm) triggers with Fire + Ice weapons in squad.

"I want this squad-wide steam effect every match."

D14

6 heroes, Master Smith cinematic at Stage 10 unlocks Forge Phase 1 (pull parts to upgrade rarity), weekly leaderboard.

Part-pull chase + leaderboard + signature-weapon mission progress.
