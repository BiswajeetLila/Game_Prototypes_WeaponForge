## What kind of video this is (and why it matters)

This is the *exact lens* you should worry about: a curator who buckets games by **how they look in a 30-second clip** — anime characters + numbers flying + "you don't have to touch anything." Every game here earns its slot by being **(a) anime-styled and (b) playable AFK**. That is the entire selection filter.

It is **not** a mechanics-aware list. The proof: Wittle Defender and Archero 2 — your two true reference competitors — are absent. To this curator they aren't "anime gacha." That cuts both ways:

- **Good:** the market already sorts arena-roguelites (Habby) and hero-gacha-JRPGs (this video) into separate buckets. You sit in the first.
- **Risk:** *your chosen skin* (anime heroes + auto-combat) is the membership badge for the **second** bucket. A reel like this could feature WeaponCraft tomorrow and it would look at home — which is the discoverability trap (see report §3).

The narrator's single most-repeated phrase, across all 11 games (~15 times): **"100% autoplay,"** "don't have to touch any button," "just leave the game." Full-AFK is the category's headline feature.

## Pattern across all 11

| Dimension | Every game in this video |
|---|---|
| **Collectible / gacha unit** | **Heroes** (summon characters; rarity stars; classes; elements) |
| **Roster** | Large, growing, pullable |
| **Combat** | Auto / idle — turn-based (E7, Outerplane), side-view party auto-battle (Terbis, OZ, Fantasy of Light), idle scroll (Dream & Lethe, Go Go Muffin), open-field auto (Dragon Traveler) |
| **Session model** | Long-haul + AFK/offline grind + daily login |
| **Structure** | Open campaign + dungeons + raids + expeditions |
| **Story** | Heavy — cutscenes, voiced characters, isekai/fantasy arcs |
| **Roguelite draft** | **None** |
| **Crafting / forge meta** | **None** |
| **Production target** | Cinematic skill VFX, AAA character art |

Not one of the eleven ships: weapon-as-the-gacha-unit, a locked story roster, a 5-minute permadeath wave-arena, a between-wave card draft, or a slot-machine forge. Those are WeaponCraft's spine.
