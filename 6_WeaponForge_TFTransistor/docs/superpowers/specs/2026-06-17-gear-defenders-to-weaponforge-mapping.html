The one structural reframe

Your assumption — "GD's gear types are my items; Function gears could be a Modifier" — is right for the connector/buff gears and needs one correction for troop gears. The correction is the whole hinge of the mapping, so it goes first.

Gear Defenders — a FACTORY

The gear grid manufactures an army. A troop-gear next to the Power Core spawns soldiers at X/s; those soldiers march off and do the fighting. Your build is a production line; the combat actors are disposable, spawned, and not the thing you collect.

→

WeaponForge — a TUNING BENCH

Each persistent hero is a frame. The Function on its emitter = what that hero shoots; adjacent Modifiers warp the shot. The combat actors are the drafted heroes you collect — not a spawned army. The forge tunes units, it doesn't manufacture them.

Consequence for the mapping: GD's troop-gear splits into our Function (the element/Active on the emitter), while GD's troops become our hero's emission/shots — not a separate item. And GD's central gate, production rate (troops/s), becomes for us a rate of shots / rate of reaction-charge (see §5). Everything else maps cleanly.

Where we deliberately diverge — the moat

Three GD design choices we intentionally do not copy. Everything in §2 marked ✳ OURS lives here. This is the entire reason to build our game instead of reskinning GD.

1. Type-triangle → cross-hero Magicka reactions

GD's combat depth is a flat unit-vs-unit bonus (Archer beats Infantry, Lancer beats Ranged). Legible, but commodity — every auto-battler has a type wheel. Our depth is elements colliding across lanes: line up a Fire hero beside a Water hero and the lanes cross-react into Steam — a screen-wide cascade. GD has no elemental reaction system at all. This is our only non-commodity asset; the type-triangle becomes a minor secondary layer (enemy weak/resist tags), and the reaction becomes the headline.

2. Production-rate gate → reaction gate

GD's gate meters raw output (troops/sec, tuned by Speed gears + tier + adjacency). If we copy that literally we optimize a commodity (DPS) and the reaction is incidental. We instead want the gate to meter time-to-next-cascade — the craft pushes toward screen-pops, not toward bigger numbers. (Open; §5.)

3. Restrained VFX → juiced payoff (selectively)

GD strips juice on purpose: no camera shake, no hit-pause, flat damage numbers — to keep the player's eye on the grid and run on cheap phones. We keep that restraint on the ordinary field (calm, legible, auto-resolving) but invert it hard for the reaction moment: the cascade must pop (slow-mo, chain-counter dopamine, crystalline cause→effect). If a cold player can't see the reaction they set up, the moat is invisible and we are a me-too. Juice is not polish here — it is the product.

The open seam: the power gate

GD's gate is production rate, tuned spatially (Speed gears + adjacency + tier). It proves a rate gate works for a casual audience — the build becomes "how much can I push per cycle." Our open question is what flows through our gate: raw power (GD-literal, rejected), the reaction (on-brand), or an economy.

Live candidates from the gating brainstorm (parked at currency-choice):

  • AP Economy (Expedition 33) — basic attacks earn AP; the reaction auto-fires when affordable. Earn-by-doing → spend-on-payoff.
  • Multiplier Gates (Mob Control / Count Masters) — on-field ×/+ gates the output crosses before it hits. Literally "gates," maximally casual.
  • Route-to-Connect (Tangle Master / Mini Metro) — charge flows along a route you set; connecting emitter → reaction-node fires it.
  • + a spatial-structure set (bridge-the-span, circuit board, marble-run, valve hub) and a dual-resource AP + team-gauge (full E33) under consideration.
Resume The gate is the one undecided organ. Sequence: pick the currency (what flows through) → pick the cadence (beat / charge / route) → fold into the craft-model doc. GD says a rate gate is casual-safe; our twist is metering the cascade, not the DPS.

Lift GD's proven skeleton — grid, merge, shop, tiers, meta. Swap the one organ that is our moat — type-triangle → cross-hero Magicka reactions + a juiced pop. Then pick our gate. Everything green is derisked by 5M installs; everything ✳ OURS is where we win or lose.

Weapon-board layouts (slots + upgrade locks)

The weapon is the board (per the §1 reframe). Four heroes, four weapon-frames — different silhouette each (matches the locked per-hero-frame call). Crucially these are not fixed colour-holes: role comes from distance to the emitter (the business end). The dashed ring = the modifier zone — a part dropped inside it warps the shot; the same part dropped outside it acts as a Passive. That preserves "one part, three roles by placement" (the thing typed slots would have killed).

Slot legend ● Active — the emitter; the element you fire (keep at 1: the cross-hero lineup is the element axis)  ·  ● Modifier — hugs the emitter; warps/speeds the shot  ·  ◯ Passive — far out; auras/stats  ·  ⊘🔒 Locked — opens by leveling the hero. The unlocks expand the MODIFIER zone (fix for the backwards economy) — leveling = your weapon shoots crazier, not "+1 stat aura."
What the four show (re: the modifier-depth point) Every frame keeps Active = 1 and grows the Modifier zone via 🔒 hero-level unlocks — so progression deepens the craft puzzle (concentrate vs spread, speed-up vs slow-down), not a stat-aura pile. The frame shape changes how modifiers arrange: Bran clusters them, Ashe lines them up, Elara rings them, the Cannon bunches few-but-heavy. Open question still on the table: Active stays 1 (recommended, protects the cross-hero lineup) vs your "3× Active" (one hero fires multiple elements — richer single hero, weaker team-lineup moat).
